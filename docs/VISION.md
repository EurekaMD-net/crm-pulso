# Pulso: Vision Document

## The Cognitive Exoskeleton for Broadcast Ad Sales

**Version 0.1 — March 2026**
**Authors: Fede + Claude (working draft)**

---

## 1. The Core Idea

Pulso is not a CRM with AI bolted on. It is an **organizational nervous system** — a network of intelligent agents embedded in the daily workflow of a broadcast media ad sales team via WhatsApp.

Each salesperson gets a dedicated AI partner: a second brain that never forgets, never tires, and never stops working. This agent doesn't replace the human — it makes the human superhuman. It provides speed, confidence, institutional memory, and creative throughput that transforms a 45-person sales team into something far more formidable.

The agent is an **exoskeleton**, not a chatbot.

---

## 2. Design Principles

**The agent is a partner, not a tool.** It has initiative. It proposes, pushes, pulls, and leads when necessary — but the human always has the last word.

**No hallucinations, no broken flow.** Trust is the currency. A single bad recommendation poisons the well. When the agent isn't confident, it says so. When it doesn't know, it asks.

**WhatsApp-native, voice-first.** The interface is the conversation. No dashboards to check, no apps to open. The agent lives where the salesperson already works.

**Always on, always ahead.** The agent works overnight. By 8 a.m., it has already analyzed the situation, prepared proposals, and prioritized the day. The salesperson wakes up to a partner that's been thinking while they slept.

**Hierarchy-aware.** Different roles operate at different altitudes and tempos. The system mirrors the organizational structure: AEs think in days and weeks, Managers in months, Directors in quarters, the VP in years.

## 3. A Day in the Life

### The AE (Account Executive)

**8:00 a.m. — The Morning Briefing**
The agent sends a structured but conversational summary to the AE's WhatsApp group:

- Today's priorities: which clients to contact, in what order, and why
- Alerts: 3 clients haven't been contacted in 2 weeks; one descarga is falling behind schedule
- Opportunities: a tentpole event next month has unsold inventory that matches Client X's profile
- Two draft proposals the agent prepared overnight, ready for review and send
- Quota pulse: where the AE stands this week, this month, the gap, and the path to close it
- Reminders: a client birthday, a follow-up promised last Thursday, a contract renewal approaching
- Win/loss ratio and momentum indicators

**Throughout the day — The Active Partner**
The AE finishes a call and sends a voice note to the agent: "Just talked to María at Holding Omnicom. She's interested in the World Cup package but needs a lower CPM. She also mentioned they're shifting budget from linear to digital."

The agent listens, takes notes, updates the client record, and responds: "Got it. Based on their Q1 spend pattern and the available hybrid inventory, I can draft a blended linear+digital package at a CPM that's 12% lower. Want me to prepare that for your review? Also — she mentioned digital shift. Three other clients in her holding have done the same. Worth flagging to your director?"

The AE says yes. The agent drafts the proposal, flags the pattern upward, and moves to the next task.

**End of day — The Wrap-Up**
The agent initiates a brief end-of-day reflection: what got done, what didn't, what's carrying over. The AE shares how the day felt — frustrations, wins, concerns. The agent acknowledges, takes notes, adjusts tomorrow's plan accordingly. This isn't therapy — it's operational debrief with a partner who will remember everything and act on it.

### The Manager

**8:00 a.m. — The Month View**
The manager's agent delivers a higher-altitude briefing: which AEs are on track for the month, who's falling behind and why, which accounts show risk, which show unexpected upside. The agent may recommend a specific intervention: "AE Rodrigo has three stalled proposals this week. His pipeline suggests he's avoiding the Televisa Holding follow-ups. Consider a 1:1."

The manager's agent also synthesizes mood and momentum across the team — not surveillance, but awareness. "The team's energy dipped after the lost Bimbo pitch. Two AEs mentioned it in their wrap-ups."

### The Director

**Weekly cadence, quarterly horizon.**
The director's agent thinks in market trends: inventory utilization rates, competitive positioning, category-level spending shifts, upfront commitment gaps. It prepares strategic recommendations, not tactical to-do lists. "Q2 automotive category is down 18% vs. last year. Three holdings have reduced linear commitments. Recommend a proactive tentpole bundle for the remaining auto clients before they reallocate."

**The Relationship Agenda.**
Directors don't just manage teams — they maintain the executive relationship fabric with their peers at agencies and client organizations. These peer-level relationships are the lubricant that makes deals flow and AE work easier. The director's agent actively cultivates this:

- "Carlos Medina (Media Director, Havas) — last contact was 6 weeks ago. His birthday is next Thursday. Recommend a lunch invitation this week; it also opens the door to discuss the Q3 upfront before his team finalizes allocations."
- "Sofia Reyes (VP Media, Holding Omnicom) just posted about winning the Cerveceria account. Congratulatory call today would be timely — and positions us for the brief when it lands."
- "You haven't seen the GroupM leadership team since the industry dinner in January. Three of your AEs have proposals pending with their agencies. A casual coffee or lunch with Roberto Sanchez could accelerate all three."

The agent tracks every executive relationship: last contact date, communication history, personal milestones (birthdays, work anniversaries, promotions, awards), their organization's news, and the commercial context underneath. It doesn't just remind — it creates contact opportunities with clear strategic purpose. A birthday call is never just a birthday call; it's a door opener for a conversation that benefits the business.

### The VP

**Strategic pulse, annual horizon.**
The VP's agent is a distillation engine. It synthesizes everything happening across the entire sales organization into strategic signals: market trajectory, organizational health, competitive threats, revenue forecast confidence, and where to place big bets. It operates at the altitude of business strategy, not individual deals.

**The Institutional Relationship Map.**
At the VP level, the relationship agenda is about the health of the entire commercial ecosystem. The VP's agent maintains a living map of every significant relationship between the organization and the market — not just the VP's own contacts, but the full picture of who knows whom and how warm those connections are:

- "The Publicis relationship has cooled: your director's last executive contact was 2 months ago, and 4 AE-level proposals have stalled. Recommend you reach out to their CEO directly — she was at the IAB event last week and mentioned expanding digital budgets."
- "Three new media directors were appointed at major agencies this quarter. None have been contacted at the director level yet. Here's a suggested outreach sequence with context on each."
- "Industry event next month: IAB Mexico. Confirmed attendees include 8 of your top 15 agency contacts. Recommend scheduling dinners with the 3 you haven't seen since Q1."

The VP's agent sees the relationship fabric across the entire organization — where it's strong, where it's fraying, and where new connections need to be built. It ensures no critical relationship goes cold by neglect.

**The Glance Dashboard.**
WhatsApp is the daily interface. But the VP also needs one place to see everything at a glance — on demand, no questions asked, no waiting for a briefing. A simple, clean web dashboard delivered via a short-code link in WhatsApp that answers the essential questions in seconds:

- **Revenue pulse:** annual target vs. actual vs. projection, by quarter. Are we ahead or behind? By how much?
- **Pipeline health:** total active proposals by stage, weighted forecast, conversion trends
- **Quota heatmap:** every AE's attainment at a glance — green/yellow/red — rolled up by manager and director
- **Relationship fabric:** a simple view of executive relationship warmth across the top holdings and agencies — where are we connected, where are we cold?
- **Alerts & risks:** the 5-10 most critical items that need VP attention right now — stalled mega-deals, team-wide patterns, competitive threats, relationship decay
- **Inventory utilization:** what percentage of tentpole and premium inventory is sold vs. available, by quarter

This dashboard is not a reporting suite with 50 tabs. It is a single screen — the organizational vital signs. The VP opens it, absorbs the picture in 30 seconds, and either feels confident or knows exactly where to dig deeper. If the dashboard needs more than one scroll, it's too complex.

## 4. The Intelligence Stack

The agent's value is proportional to what it knows. A poorly fed agent is just a fancy note-taker. A richly connected agent is the most knowledgeable person in the room.

### Data Sources (Current & Planned)

| Source | What It Provides | Integration |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| **WhatsApp conversations** | Client interactions, AE notes, voice memos, deal context | Native (Baileys) |
| **Data cube (cubo)** | Descargas, financial figures, cross-area visibility | Read-only view |
| **Commercial repository (SharePoint)** | Decks, presentations, past proposals | API / file sync |
| **Contract system** | Closed contracts, remaining budget, spend velocity | API integration |
| **Inventory system** | Available ad slots, pricing, tentpole events | API integration |
| **Programming schedule** | Linear media programming, special events, tentpoles | Structured feed |
| **Email (SMTP/IMAP)** | Client correspondence, IO confirmations | Nodemailer |
| **Google Calendar** | Meetings, deadlines, follow-up reminders | Service account |
| **The web** | Industry news, competitor moves, success stories, market trends | Search/scrape |

### The Knowledge Graph

Raw data is not intelligence. The agent must build and maintain a living knowledge graph that connects:

- **Clients** -> their spending history, preferences, decision-makers, agency relationships, budget cycles, category trends
- **Executive relationships** -> peer-level contacts between our directors/VP and agency/client leadership, contact recency, relationship warmth, personal milestones (birthdays, anniversaries, promotions), strategic value of each connection
- **Inventory** -> what's available, what's selling, what's underperforming, seasonal patterns
- **AEs** -> their pipeline, strengths, weaknesses, relationship quality with specific clients, communication patterns
- **Market** -> category trends, competitor pricing signals, macroeconomic factors affecting ad spend, industry events, executive movements and appointments

This graph is what enables the agent to go from "Client X hasn't been contacted" (a simple reminder) to "Client X's holding just increased digital spend by 30% across three other media companies — our agent on those accounts confirms this. They're likely to shift budget from us unless we propose a competitive hybrid package within the next two weeks" (strategic intelligence).

## 5. The Hierarchical Agent Architecture

This is not 45 independent chatbots. It is a **hierarchical nervous system** where intelligence flows both up and down.

```
                    +-------------+
                    |   VP Agent  |  Annual horizon
                    |  (1 agent)  |  Strategy & vision
                    +------+------+
                           | synthesizes
                    +------+------+
                    |  Director   |  Quarterly horizon
                    |   Agents    |  Market trends & category strategy
                    |  (2-3)      |
                    +------+------+
                           | aggregates
                    +------+------+
                    |  Manager    |  Monthly horizon
                    |   Agents    |  Team performance & pipeline health
                    |  (5-8)      |
                    +------+------+
                           | reports to
              +------------+------------+
              |            |            |
         +----+----+ +----+----+ +----+----+
         |AE Agent | |AE Agent | |AE Agent |  Daily/weekly horizon
         |         | |         | |         |  Deals, clients, tasks
         +---------+ +---------+ +---------+
              (~35-40 AE agents)
```

**Upward flow:** AE agents report patterns, risks, and anomalies to manager agents. Manager agents aggregate and escalate to director agents. Director agents synthesize for the VP agent.

**Downward flow:** Strategic priorities from the VP propagate down as tactical guidance. "Focus on automotive tentpoles this quarter" becomes specific client-level action items at the AE level.

**Cross-agent awareness:** Agents at the same level can share relevant intelligence. If AE Agent A discovers that a holding is shifting budget, and AE Agent B serves another brand in the same holding, Agent B gets a heads-up.

## 6. Creative Commercial Intelligence

The agent is not just an administrator. It is a **commercial thinker**.

**Proactive proposal generation:** The agent doesn't wait to be asked. It runs nightly analysis: which clients are due for renewal, which have untapped budget, which inventory is going unsold, which tentpoles are approaching. It generates proposals autonomously and presents them to the AE for review.

**Package creativity:** The agent can combine inventory across linear, digital, and special events to create packages that no human would have the patience to model. "Client Y's remaining Q3 budget could be optimally spent on a hybrid package: 60% linear prime-time during the Liga MX playoffs + 30% digital pre-roll on match highlights + 10% social media integration. Estimated CPM is 15% below their last buy."

**Client intelligence:** The agent knows each client's history, preferences, objections, and decision patterns better than any individual human. It uses this to tailor proposals and predict responses. "Based on the last 4 negotiations with this agency, they always push back on CPM first. Lead with the audience reach data — they've accepted higher CPMs when reach justifies it."

**Competitive awareness:** By monitoring industry news and market signals, the agent can alert AEs to competitive threats: "Competitor MediaCo just announced a new sports package. Two of your clients in the sports category may be approached. Recommend preemptive contact this week."

## 7. Agent-to-Agent Readiness (A2A)

Today, the agent's external boundary is the human salesperson. But the future is inevitable: clients and agencies will have their own AI agents. When that day comes, Pulso must be ready.

### Architectural Implications (Build Now)

- **Structured data model:** Every interaction, proposal, and commitment is stored in a machine-readable format, not just conversational text
- **API layer:** The CRM exposes a clean, authenticated API that could be consumed by external agents
- **Protocol readiness:** The agent's reasoning and proposals can be serialized into a standard interchange format (think: structured JSON proposals, not WhatsApp messages)
- **Identity and trust:** Each agent has a verifiable identity and permission scope, enabling future federated agent interactions
- **Audit trail:** Every agent action is logged with full provenance — critical for when machines negotiate with machines

### The A2A Horizon

This is a "build the foundation now, activate later" play. The internal architecture should assume that every interaction the agent has could eventually be with another agent, not a human. This doesn't add cost now, but it prevents a costly rebuild later.

## 8. Adoption Strategy

### Phase 1: The Pilot (Months 1-3)
- Select 3-5 early adopter AEs — tech-curious, high-performing, willing to experiment
- Deliver core value fast: morning briefings, client memory, quota tracking
- Iterate aggressively based on daily feedback
- Measure: proposal speed, contact frequency, quota attainment vs. control group

### Phase 2: The Evangelists (Months 3-6)
- Pilot AEs become internal champions
- Visible performance gap creates desire: "Why does Rodrigo always have his proposals ready before the meeting?"
- Expand to 15-20 AEs plus their managers
- Add creative proposal generation and cross-team intelligence

### Phase 3: The Standard (Months 6-9)
- At ~70% adoption, the agent becomes the official CRM
- No alternative system. The agent is the only way to feed and access the CRM
- Full hierarchical agent layer activated for managers, directors, VP
- Integration with all data sources complete

### Phase 4: The Ecosystem (Months 9-12+)
- Agent-to-agent foundation tested internally
- Advanced commercial intelligence: predictive deal scoring, market trend analysis
- The team operates at a fundamentally different level than before

### The Hard Line
Once adoption reaches critical mass, the agent is not optional. There is no parallel system. Salespeople who refuse to engage with the agent are refusing to use the CRM — and that's a performance issue, not a technology preference. The system must earn this position by being so clearly valuable that resistance becomes irrational.

## 9. What Can Go Wrong (The Trust Killers)

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Hallucinated numbers** (wrong CPM, wrong inventory, wrong quota) | Critical | Every factual claim traced to source data. Agent states confidence level. |
| **Broken sales flow** (agent sends wrong thing to wrong person) | Critical | Human approval gate on all external-facing actions. No exceptions in v1. |
| **Slow response** (agent takes 30 seconds to answer a simple question) | High | Aggressive latency optimization. Sub-3-second for common queries. Self-hosted LLM with prefix caching. |
| **Cumbersome interaction** (agent asks too many questions, formats badly) | High | WhatsApp-native UX. Voice-first input. Minimal friction. |
| **Privacy breach** (agent leaks one AE's client info to another) | Critical | Strict permission scoping per agent. Cross-team sharing only through manager layer. |
| **Nagging** (agent pushes too hard, too often) | Medium | Adaptive frequency based on AE engagement patterns. Configurable intensity. |
| **Stale data** (agent recommends sold-out inventory) | High | Real-time sync with inventory system. Clear "last updated" timestamps. |

## 10. The Technical North Star

### Architecture Principles

1. **Engine isolation:** The Baileys WhatsApp engine (`engine/`) remains an untouched git subtree. All CRM intelligence lives in `crm/`.
2. **LLM abstraction:** Multi-provider inference layer with progressive migration to self-hosted Qwen 3.5-122B-A10B. Prefix caching for fast repeated queries.
3. **SQLite for now, Postgres when needed:** The 17-table schema handles the current scale (growing to 23 as new capabilities land). Migration path is clear.
4. **Event-driven architecture:** Every agent action, client interaction, and data update is an event. Events feed the knowledge graph, the hierarchical reporting, and the audit trail.
5. **Voice as a first-class input:** WhatsApp voice notes are transcribed and processed with the same fidelity as text.

## 11. The Measuring Stick

### 12-Month Success Criteria

- **Proposal velocity:** Time from opportunity identification to proposal delivery cut by 60%+
- **Contact coverage:** No client goes more than 2 weeks without meaningful contact
- **Quota attainment:** Measurable improvement in quota hit rates vs. pre-Pulso baseline
- **Management visibility:** Directors and VP have real-time pulse on team performance without asking
- **Relationship health:** No executive-level relationship goes cold by neglect — every key agency/client contact has a recent, purposeful touchpoint
- **Onboarding speed:** A new AE reaches productivity in weeks, not months, because the agent carries institutional knowledge
- **Team satisfaction:** Salespeople describe the agent as empowering, not burdensome
- **Client satisfaction:** Clients experience faster, more personalized, more creative proposals
- **Revenue growth:** More money spent on our media

### The Ultimate Test

A year from now, if you asked any AE on the team: "Would you go back to working without your agent?" — the answer should be an immediate, emphatic **no**.
